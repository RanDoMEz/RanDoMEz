“He wears his heart out on his sleeve,” she wailed, “where everyone can see!”
“You have no need to feel aggrieved,” I said, “no one reads poetry.”
“But what if,” she persisted, “but what if they did? He leaves it out
And doesn't even check it. What if someone steals it? What about
If it grows stale? If it goes bad? Or rots or dies or falls apart
And spawns anew in its entrails a colony of mutant hearts - “
I sigh. “my dear, do you believe the man has nothing up his sleeve?”
-Making Love With Scrabble Tiles, Joshua Ip
